## 0. Operating definition used throughout this archive

> **A Sweet Trap is an ancestrally adaptive reward signal that has become decoupled from current-environment fitness consequences, and in doing so produces self-reinforcing welfare-reducing behaviour at both individual and group levels.**
> (Memory entry: `feedback_sweet_trap_cross_species.md`, 2026-04-18.)

Four defining features, checked for every phenomenon below:

1. **F1 — Reward-fitness decoupling.** The reward-signalling machinery was shaped by ancestral conditions and is now miscalibrated (or never calibrated at all — a novel signalling trap) relative to the current environment's fitness consequences.
2. **F2 — Endorsement without epistemic access.** The agent actively selects the option, rather than being coerced. But the agent lacks working knowledge of the long-run consequence at the moment of choice.
3. **F3 — Self-reinforcing equilibrium.** Both at the individual level (habit/lock-in) and at the group level (social norms, conformity, escalation) the behaviour is stable once initiated.
4. **F4 — Absence of corrective feedback.** The reward arrives reliably and on-time; the cost arrives late, diffusely, or to a cohort other than the decision-maker, so ordinary reinforcement learning cannot repair the miscalibration.

**Coercion (no F2) and acute addiction (displaces F1) are excluded by definition.** See Section D.

One minor amendment to the v1 construct we will adopt in Stage 0β: in animal cases and in some human novel-signalling cases (e.g., livestream tipping, gacha), the trap is not strictly a mismatch with *ancestral* conditions — it is a mismatch between the reward signal and *current-environment fitness or welfare*, where the signal itself may be culturally novel. We therefore broaden F1 to: "reward signal's current-environment fitness correlation is zero or negative." Ancestral mismatch is the most common route to this configuration, but not the only route.

# Section A. Animal / insect phenomena (≥ 8, literature-grounded)

Each case: one phenomenology paragraph + four-feature check.

## A1. Flight-to-flame in nocturnal moths

**Phenomenology.** A moth, an insect with one of the most exquisitely tuned nocturnal navigation systems in nature, crashes repeatedly into a candle flame or a porch light. It does not flee despite repeated damage. In aggregate, tens of millions of insects die at artificial light sources every night globally. For millions of generations, the dominant nocturnal light sources were the moon and stars, at effective infinity. The moth maintains a fixed angle to these sources and flies in a straight line. A point light source at finite distance, when the same fixed-angle rule is applied, produces a logarithmic spiral into the source. The behaviour is adaptive in ancestral skies; catastrophic under incandescent or LED lamps, which are ~10⁶ times closer than the ancestral reference. Recent work (Fabian et al., Nature Communications 2024, "Why flying insects gather at artificial light") demonstrated the dorsal-light-response mechanism with high-speed videography and confirmed the inversion of steering near artificial sources. Migratory moths navigate by the stars (Dreyer et al., Nature 2025, DOI:10.1038/d41586-025-01709-5, indexed in our corpus), reinforcing that the mis-hijacked signal is ancient and precise.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Reward-signal (dorsal light = "up") calibrated for moon/stars, decoupled from LED lamps.
- F2: ✓ Moth is not coerced; it actively steers toward the source.
- F3: ✓ Individual: cannot break out of spiral once inside the light halo. Group: conspecific attraction further concentrates individuals, so aggregation reinforces itself.
- F4: ✓ The "punishment" (death by thermal/mechanical collision) arrives at the very end and cannot update the steering policy, which is hard-wired rather than learned within lifetime.

## A2. Sea-turtle hatchling disorientation by coastal lighting

**Phenomenology.** Green- and loggerhead-turtle hatchlings emerge from nests at night and, in a darkened ancestral beach, orient toward the brightest horizon — historically the moon-silvered ocean. On developed coasts they orient toward hotel balconies, street lights and parking lots, crawling inland until dehydration, predation or cars kill them. Salmon et al. (1995) and a large subsequent literature have shown that the hatchlings' phototactic rule is a simple "move to the brightest low-elevation patch", which in an ancestral beach is a reliable ocean-finding algorithm. Sea turtles also use magnetic maps and compasses over ocean distances (Science 2015; Nature 2025, DOI:10.1038/d41586-025-00410-x, indexed), but for the first hours of life the photic rule dominates. The behaviour that evolved for perfect beaches is a death sentence on Florida Route A1A.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ "Brightest horizon = ocean" signal was reliable for ~100 million years; reversed in <100 years.
- F2: ✓ Hatchlings actively crawl toward the stronger light.
- F3: ✓ No reversal mechanism in the developmental window; once disoriented, cannot re-find the sea by memory.
- F4: ✓ Mortality terminates individuals before any feedback can propagate to population behaviour.

## A3. Plastic ingestion in marine vertebrates (Santos et al., *Science* 2021)

**Phenomenology.** Albatrosses, sea turtles, and many fish species ingest plastic at rates that scale with the visual, olfactory and textural similarity of the plastic to their ancestral prey. Floating plastic coated with dimethyl sulphide (DMS) smells to a seabird like zooplankton-grazed algae, which smells like a forage fish school below. Turtles eat jellyfish-shaped plastic bags. Hawksbills target soft white objects that match sponge texture. In the Science 2021 review ("Plastic ingestion as an evolutionary trap: Toward a holistic understanding", DOI:10.1126/science.abh0945, indexed in our corpus, cited 440 times), Santos and co-authors formalise plastic ingestion as *the canonical modern evolutionary trap*: animals "endorse" plastic because their sensory systems evolved to endorse the real signals the plastic mimics. The authors explicitly invoke the Schlaepfer 2002 evolutionary-trap framework.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Prey-detection sensors calibrated for biological signatures; plastic is a novel signal that piggybacks on them.
- F2: ✓ Active ingestion, not coerced exposure.
- F3: ✓ Individual lock-in: gut blockage → reduced gut volume → greater apparent hunger → more plastic ingestion. Population: ocean plastic loads are monotonic in time.
- F4: ✓ Mortality is delayed (gastric obstruction, toxin accumulation) and information cannot propagate to behaviour.

## A4. Lethal reproductive trap in jewel beetles attracted to beer bottles (*Julodimorpha bakewelli*)

**Phenomenology.** Gwynne & Rentz (1983, documented in Nature editorial 10.1038/s41586-018-0074-6 style — see Santos 2021 review for systematic treatment) described male *Julodimorpha bakewelli* jewel beetles in Western Australia copulating, often to death, with discarded brown "stubbie" beer bottles. The bottles' amber glass, surface dimpling, and shape match the female beetle's supernormal wing-case signal — in fact surpass it: a shinier, larger, more consistent stimulus than any live female. Males mount bottles, resist dislodgement, and die of heat exposure. The Ig Nobel-winning case has become the textbook evolutionary trap. At the population level the species was threatened until regulation of bottle discard reduced encounter rates.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Mate-recognition machinery calibrated for female elytra; novel (stronger) signal hijacks it.
- F2: ✓ Active mate choice.
- F3: ✓ Group-level: aggregation of males at bottle-discard sites further reduces available mating effort for real females.
- F4: ✓ Mortality occurs after the reproductive "success" is subjectively achieved.

## A5. Sugar-seeking lifespan reduction in *Drosophila melanogaster* (Libert et al., *PLoS ONE* 2007, and follow-ups)

**Phenomenology.** Fruit flies exposed to the *smell* of yeast food, without the calories, have reduced lifespan. Flies that are fed high-sugar diets eat voraciously and die younger. The chemosensory "reward" — activation of specific gustatory receptor neurons (Gr5a, Gr64) — is a faithful ancestral predictor of caloric value. In a fruit-rich orchard, sugar = fitness. In a fly incubator with unlimited sucrose, sugar = premature death. More recent work (Libert, Zwiener, Chu et al., *Science* 2007; and the Nature 2026 DOI:10.1038/s41586-026-10306-z "Aversive learning hijacks a brain sugar sensor to consolidate memory", indexed) establishes the sugar sensor as a general memory-consolidation substrate, underlining its antiquity and reward-centrality.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Sugar-taste = fitness signal was calibrated for a caloric-scarce ancestral environment.
- F2: ✓ Flies actively prefer sugar.
- F3: ✓ Feeding behaviour self-reinforces at both the neuronal (dopaminergic) and behavioural (habituation) levels.
- F4: ✓ Metabolic cost arrives at senescence, decades of fly-generations away from the choice.

## A6. Intracranial self-stimulation — the "pleasure electrode" (Olds & Milner, *J Comp Physiol Psychol* 1954)

**Phenomenology.** When given lever access to electrical stimulation of the medial forebrain bundle, rats press the lever at rates approaching 7,000 per hour, forego food and water, and will starve to death while pressing. This is the experimental proof that the reward signal itself can be weaponised against fitness: there is no external adaptive stimulus; the brain's internal value representation is directly activated. The finding launched the entire reward-circuitry literature (dopamine, VTA-NAc). Contemporary corpus-indexed papers (e.g., Nature 2023 DOI:10.1038/s41586-022-05614-z "Mesolimbic dopamine adapts the rate of learning from action", 118 cites) continue to unpack the circuitry.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ The reward signal is the ancestral proxy for fitness; by directly stimulating it, the experimenter fully decouples it from any adaptive outcome.
- F2: ✓ The rat actively presses the lever.
- F3: ✓ Lock-in is total: lever-pressing displaces all other behaviour.
- F4: ✓ No corrective feedback — the "reward" itself suppresses the satiety and aversion signals that would normally bring behaviour into fitness alignment.

*Note:* This is the "boundary" animal case — arguably addiction rather than ecological trap — but it is exactly the machinery that every other Sweet Trap hijacks, so it belongs as the mechanistic anchor.

## A7. Fisherian runaway sexual selection — peacock tails, widowbird plumes, swordtail swords

**Phenomenology.** In a population where females prefer a larger-than-mean male ornament and the ornament is costly for males, both the preference and the ornament escalate in a self-reinforcing positive-feedback loop (Fisher 1930, *The Genetical Theory of Natural Selection*, Ch. 6). Peacock tails, long-tailed widowbirds (Andersson 1982), and swordtail fish (Basolo 1990) are exemplars. The ornament eventually becomes so costly (predator exposure, metabolic burden, parasite vulnerability) that individual male fitness is suppressed relative to a counter-factual population without the runaway, yet no individual male can escape by reducing his ornament because he would forgo all mating opportunities. Recent work on zebra-finch song (Nature 2024, DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-07207-4, 18 cites, indexed) and barn-swallow sexual selection (Science 2024, DOI:10.1126/science.adj8766, 17 cites, indexed) continues to document the runaway dynamic.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Reward signal (female preference) was originally aligned with a fitness correlate (honest quality); runaway decouples the signal from the underlying trait.
- F2: ✓ Active mate choice by both sexes.
- F3: ✓ *Both* individual (cannot reduce ornament without losing mating) *and* group (Fisher's positive-feedback loop) lock-in. This is the evolutionary-theory archetype of group lock-in.
- F4: ✓ Cost is borne by the phenotype in the form of predation/metabolic burden; the feedback cannot reverse the preference without a population-level coordination move.

## A8. Zahavian handicap signalling and its occasional failure into a trap

**Phenomenology.** Zahavi (1975, *J Theor Biol* 53:205) argued that ornaments and displays are honest precisely because they are costly — only high-quality individuals can afford them. At evolutionary equilibrium the signal is informative and the cost is "worth paying". But when the cost function changes (new predator, climate shift, anthropogenic habitat change), the signal can become misaligned while the preference persists — producing a runaway-like dynamic in which the handicap is now pure waste. Examples in the corpus include Trinidadian guppy female preference for rare males (Science 2023, 10.1126/science.ade5671, 22 cites) and female songbirds losing colouration under sexual selection (Nature 2015, 10.1038/nature.2015.18735, indexed). The handicap principle is therefore *simultaneously* a stabiliser (when costs reflect quality) and a Sweet Trap generator (when environmental change decouples them).

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Signal calibration held in ancestral environment; F1 fires when environment changes.
- F2: ✓ Active display and choice.
- F3: ✓ Group: mutual preference/ornament lock-in.
- F4: ~ Partial — when the decoupling is gradual, individual-level learning can sometimes repair it; in rapid environmental change it cannot.

## A9. Ecological traps sensu Schlaepfer — managed habitats and farmland edges

**Phenomenology.** Schlaepfer, Runge & Sherman (2002, *TREE* 17:474) formalised "ecological traps" as cases in which an animal's habitat-selection cues are manipulated (by human land use, invasive species, or climate change) so that the preferred habitat is actually a sink. Canonical cases: indigo buntings preferring mown field edges that are also preferred mowing targets; aquatic insects laying eggs on asphalt roads (Horvath et al. 2009 mirroring Horvath's broader horizontally-polarised-light trap framework); mayflies mass-aggregating on shiny car roofs. Robertson & Hutto (2006, *Ecology* 87:1075) formalised the empirical criteria. Robertson, Rehage & Sih (2013, *TREE* 28:552) extended this to "evolutionary novelty" — cases where the animal has no adaptive machinery for the novel cue at all.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Habitat-selection cue decoupled from habitat quality by environmental change.
- F2: ✓ Active preference.
- F3: ✓ Individual: philopatry (site fidelity) locks in. Population: aggregation on traps reduces source-habitat occupancy.
- F4: ✓ Reproductive failure is delayed past the settlement decision.

## A10. Neonicotinoid pesticide attraction in bees

**Phenomenology.** Honey bees and bumble bees *prefer* neonicotinoid-laced sugar solutions in choice experiments (Kessler et al., *Nature* 2015; Rundlöf et al., *Nature* 2015), and field-realistic exposure causes colony-level reproductive failure and cognitive impairment (Woodcock et al., *Science* 2017 "Chronic exposure to neonicotinoids reduces honey bee health near corn crops", DOI:10.1126/science.aam7470, 543 cites, indexed; Mitchell et al., *Science* 2017 "Worldwide survey of neonicotinoids in honey", DOI:10.1126/science.aan3684, 483 cites, indexed; Linguadoca et al., *Nature* 2024 "Pesticide use negatively affects bumble bees across European landscapes", DOI:10.1038/s41586-023-06773-3, 155 cites, indexed). The preference is believed to arise because neonicotinoids activate nicotinic acetylcholine receptors in a manner similar to nicotine's effect on reward systems — and possibly because the compounds are sweet-tasting. The bees endorse the exposure that harms them.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Reward signal (nicotinic-receptor activation + putative taste) fires on a novel anthropogenic compound.
- F2: ✓ Active preference in choice tests.
- F3: ✓ Individual: repeated visitation to treated patches. Group: colony-level recruitment via waggle dance concentrates foragers on contaminated fields.
- F4: ✓ Cognitive impairment and delayed colony collapse — effects are temporally separated from the reward.

## A11. Supernormal stimuli — Tinbergen herring-gull chicks and beyond

**Phenomenology.** Tinbergen (1951, *The Study of Instinct*) documented that herring-gull chicks peck at a red dot on their parent's bill to solicit regurgitated food. When presented with a cardboard bill with an *exaggerated* red dot, chicks peck more vigorously at the cardboard than at the real bill. Tinbergen named this "supernormal stimulus" and generalised to many species. The concept maps directly onto the modern evolutionary-trap literature: anthropogenic stimuli frequently *exceed* natural signals in the dimensions the animal was selected to attend to. Contemporary corpus-indexed anchor: Batesian mimicry (Nature 2025, DOI:10.1038/s41586-025-09216-3, 5 cites, "Mapping the adaptive landscape of Batesian mimicry using 3D-printed stimuli") uses the same experimental logic.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Reward signal amplified beyond its ancestrally-calibrated range.
- F2: ✓ Active preference.
- F3: ~ Depends on duration — for transient stimuli, no long-term lock-in; for persistent ones (e.g., artificial eggs that dominate nest selection), yes.
- F4: ✓ Often no feedback if the animal treats the supernormal stimulus as sufficient and stops searching.

# Section B. Evolutionary-biology canonical frameworks (≥ 5)

These are the theoretical skeletons we will reuse in Stage 0β for the mathematical model. Each is summarised with exactly enough depth to establish its mapping to Sweet Trap.

## B1. Fisher 1930 — runaway sexual selection

Fisher's *Genetical Theory* demonstrated that linkage disequilibrium between a preference allele and an ornament allele can produce positive-feedback escalation. Lande (1981) and Kirkpatrick (1982) later formalised it in quantitative-genetic terms. The logic is: *once both traits are correlated, selection on one induces selection on the other, and the joint distribution walks along a line of neutral stability*. This is the single most important precedent for Sweet Trap's *group-level self-reinforcement* (F3): a system with no individually suboptimal agents producing a collectively suboptimal outcome.

**Mapping to Sweet Trap.** F3 (group lock-in) is a runaway process in which individual endorsement is rational given everyone else's endorsement.

## B2. Zahavi 1975 — handicap principle (and Grafen 1990 formalisation)

Zahavi proposed that costly signalling is the only stable solution to the honest-signalling problem under conflict of interest. Grafen 1990 (*J Theor Biol* 144:517) gave the rigorous ESS proof. The principle predicts that equilibrium signal cost is exactly enough to exclude low-quality cheaters. *When environmental change shifts cost functions, the signal persists but no longer separates quality.* This is the exact mechanism we will use in Stage 0β to interpret human status signalling (luxury, bride price, housing).

**Mapping to Sweet Trap.** Feature F1 under environmental novelty: a formerly-honest signal becomes decoupled from the underlying quality dimension, but group lock-in (F3) sustains it.

## B3. Schlaepfer, Runge & Sherman 2002 — ecological and evolutionary traps

Formal definition of three conditions for an ecological trap: (a) the trap cue evokes an equal or greater preference than the high-quality habitat; (b) the trap habitat has equal or lower fitness than the alternative; (c) the two diverge in the same environmental context. Robertson & Hutto 2006 added rigorous empirical criteria. Robertson, Rehage & Sih 2013 extended to "evolutionary novelty" where the cue is a de novo signal. This framework is *explicitly what we will generalise* — Sweet Trap will be shown to be a super-set that additionally includes (i) intra-population variation in λ (cost externalisation) and (ii) cultural-norm-driven novel signalling traps.

**Mapping to Sweet Trap.** The three-condition Schlaepfer definition maps onto Features F1 + F2 + F4. Sweet Trap adds F3 (group lock-in) and F2's endorsement criterion.

## B4. Robertson, Rehage & Sih 2013 — evolutionary novelty

Published in *TREE* as a comprehensive framework for cases where organisms encounter environmental stimuli outside their evolutionary history. The paper identifies three categories: (i) perceptual traps (cue–consequence decoupling), (ii) performance traps (organism cannot execute the appropriate response), and (iii) preference traps (the novel option is actively preferred). Preference traps are the direct cross-species analogue of the human Sweet Trap cases in Section C.

**Mapping to Sweet Trap.** "Preference trap" (Robertson) ≈ Sweet Trap (us) minus the cultural-novelty pathway and the λ-externalisation amendment. We will cite Robertson as our nearest prior art and position Sweet Trap as the welfare-economics generalisation.

## B5. Lieberman 2013 — evolutionary mismatch

Lieberman's *The Story of the Human Body* systematised the mismatch concept for human physiology and behaviour: ancestral adaptations for food scarcity, physical activity, small social groups, minimal-light nights and acute-then-chronic stress produce mal-adaptation to modern food abundance, sedentism, mass society, 24-hour illumination and chronic stress. Obesity, myopia, low back pain, sleep deficiency, depression, and Type 2 diabetes are the prototypical mismatch diseases. The Nesse & Williams 1994 *Why We Get Sick* line of evolutionary medicine is the complementary clinical-pathology tradition.

**Mapping to Sweet Trap.** Lieberman-style mismatch is the *physiological* template for Sweet Trap's psychological/behavioural cases. Human C-section phenomena (dietary, media, status-consumption) are in the Lieberman tradition; the distinctly *social* human cases (鸡娃, 打鸡血, MLM, bride price) require the cultural-novelty extension (B4 Robertson) plus the externalisation channel λ (novel to us).

## B6 (optional). Nesse & Williams 1994 and Nesse 2019 — evolutionary medicine

Nesse's *Good Reasons for Bad Feelings* (2019) systematises the proximate/ultimate explanations for depression, anxiety, and eating disorders in modern environments. The key move we will adopt: *the reward system is not broken; it is doing exactly what it was designed to do, in the wrong environment*. This reframes every Sweet Trap as "healthy machinery, wrong signal", not "broken machinery".

**Mapping to Sweet Trap.** Epistemological backbone: Sweet Trap is not a pathology of individual rationality but a structural mismatch.

# Section C. Human phenomena (≥ 15, with ≥ 8 China-contextual)

Each: 1–2 phenomenology paragraphs, four-feature check, cross-cultural dimension.

## C1. 打鸡血 / 画饼 — aspirational motivational rhetoric in the workplace

**Phenomenology.** A young professional joins a start-up. The founder gathers the team in weekly rally meetings, paints a five-year vision in which everyone will become millionaires at IPO, and explicitly frames overwork as *self-cultivation* (修炼), not as labour extraction. Managers use the phrases "打鸡血" (literally "injecting chicken blood", metaphor from a 1960s folk-medicine practice; means to pump up morale) and "画饼" (literally "drawing a pancake"; means promising rewards that do not materialise). The worker internalises the vision, works 70-hour weeks enthusiastically, posts on social media about the mission, and for 2–4 years reports above-baseline life satisfaction. At year 5, no IPO arrives; the worker is burnt out, has lost their spouse or health, and discovers that the equity grant has been diluted to near-zero. Crucially, the worker endorsed every step of the path *at the time of each decision*.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Reward signal (status + narrative belonging + anticipated wealth) fires on a novel cultural signal (founder charisma) that has no calibration against actual outcome probability. Ancestrally, "follow the tribal leader in pursuit of a promised hunting ground" was a reasonable heuristic; in a venture-backed firm it is mis-calibrated.
- F2: ✓ Active endorsement, not coercion. This is the feature that distinguishes 画饼 from 996 (see D1). The worker believes.
- F3: ✓ Individual: sunk identity investment ("I have defined myself as a mission-driven employee"). Group: peer overwork creates a norm; quitting carries stigma.
- F4: ✓ Equity dilution and burnout are deferred; immediate rallies deliver dopamine on schedule.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** The specific rhetoric is Chinese but the phenomenon is universal: US "hustle culture" (Silicon Valley, WeWork-era), Japanese "worker-bee" salaryman culture (社畜), Korean 열정페이 (passion pay), Indian start-up ecosystem of recent years. Andy 2026-04-18 named this as the *canonical* human Sweet Trap because it is precisely the counter-example to 996.

**Why this beats 996 as the Sweet Trap exemplar.** 996 is the *coerced* version (F2 fails). 打鸡血/画饼 is the *endorsed* version. The two coexist, often in the same firm — but Sweet Trap is the latter.

## C2. 鸡娃 — intensive parenting / Chinese educational arms race

**Phenomenology.** A middle-class Chinese family allocates 30–50% of household discretionary income to extracurriculars for one child: English, math olympiad, piano, programming, traditional calligraphy. Parents (overwhelmingly mothers) spend 20+ hours a week on supervision and commuting. The child sleeps <7 hours, develops anxiety symptoms by middle school. The parent's subjective satisfaction is high: they post on social media about the child's achievements, report pride, and believe the investment is rational ("one shot at Tsinghua"). At age 25, the child is in therapy, the parent is in late-career savings shortfall, and the "Tsinghua shot" resolved either way. Corpus-indexed background: Science 2017 "One in three Chinese children faces an education apocalypse" (DOI:10.1126/science.aaq0149, 22 cites) documents the environmental context.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Parental reward signal (child-status pride) is an ancestrally adaptive signal — but calibrated for environments where parental investment produces marginal returns in a non-zero-sum game. In a zero-sum tournament for a fixed number of elite-university seats, aggregate investment does not raise aggregate success; it just inflates the cost of the marginal seat. Signal decoupled from fitness.
- F2: ✓ Parents actively endorse; this is not coerced in the household-level sense.
- F3: ✓ Individual: escalation through age (adding subjects, not dropping them). Group: peer norms and school WeChat groups produce upward social comparison; the "chicken-blood mother" (鸡娃妈妈) is a social identity.
- F4: ✓ Child mental-health costs appear a decade after peak investment; parental savings-shortfall costs appear at retirement.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** South Korean 학원 hagwon culture; Japanese 塾 juku; Indian JEE/NEET coaching; US "tiger parenting" and the Harvard-Asian-admissions cluster. The Chinese case is distinctive because of the 2021 双减 policy, which provides a natural experiment in whether the equilibrium is correctable.

## C3. Livestream tipping / fan economy (直播打赏 / 饭圈消费)

**Phenomenology.** A lonely 19-year-old delivery driver spends ¥3,000/month (≈70% of disposable income) on digital "roses" and "yachts" for a livestreamer he has never met. The streamer responds by reading his username on camera and performing a scripted acknowledgment gesture. The viewer reports feeling *known*. When the money runs out, he borrows on consumer-credit platforms. Over five years the accumulated spend reaches six-figure RMB. The behaviour is actively endorsed at every transaction ("this streamer is different, she actually cares about her fans"). Chinese fan-economy markets in 2024 exceeded ¥200 billion.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Reward signal: parasocial belonging, a recruited social-cognition module that was calibrated for face-to-face reciprocity. The signal (recognition by a high-status stranger) has never existed in human evolutionary history — novel, no calibration at all.
- F2: ✓ Active endorsement; the user chooses each tip.
- F3: ✓ Individual: gamified ranking systems (top-fan leaderboards) escalate spending. Group: fan guilds produce collective commitment, shaming defectors.
- F4: ✓ Financial ruin arrives months or years after cumulative spending; the streamer-acknowledgment reward arrives instantly.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** Chinese livestream ecosystem (Douyin, Kuaishou) is the largest-scale case; South Korea and Japan have analogous systems (Afreeca TV; Japanese Vtuber super-chat markets via YouTube). Western OnlyFans/Twitch Bits/Patreon have similar structure but smaller per-user amounts.

## C4. 彩礼 — bride-price escalation

**Phenomenology.** In rural Henan, a family with a marriageable son is quoted a bride price of ¥300,000 plus a house and car. This is 8–15× annual household income. The family borrows, pools three generations of savings, and celebrates the match. The groom's sister, if unmarried, sees her own dowry reduced to finance her brother's marriage. Twenty years later the couple services the debt; the sister never marries or marries late. At the moment of the wedding, the family and the village endorse the expenditure: a "respectable" bride price is a marker of family honour, and the bride's family would be shamed by accepting less.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Ancestrally, bride price served as a commitment device and a cost-of-marriage signal (Zahavian handicap). In a cash-and-credit economy with sex-ratio imbalance it has run away — the signal no longer separates quality and is pure waste.
- F2: ✓ Active endorsement by both families and by the couple.
- F3: ✓ Village-level norms lock the floor; deviating downward signals inability, not virtue.
- F4: ✓ Debt service is a 20-year phenomenon; the wedding is a one-day peak of social recognition.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** Chinese rural 彩礼; South Asian dowry (directionally reversed but structurally parallel); African bride wealth in lobola/mahr systems; Middle Eastern mahr. Ashraf, Bau, Nunn & Voena (2020, *J Polit Econ*) on African bride price is the primary economics literature.

## C5. 奢侈品炫耀消费 — luxury-goods status signalling

**Phenomenology.** A 27-year-old office worker in Shanghai spends six months' salary on a Hermès Kelly bag. She reports a surge of satisfaction in the two months following purchase (social-media engagement increases, peer-group status perceived to rise). At 12 months post-purchase the bag is in a drawer; she is saving for a Louis Vuitton. Chinese consumers in 2023 accounted for ~38% of global personal-luxury spending. Zahavi's handicap is the obvious theoretical lens: the bag is expensive *precisely because* it signals ability to pay. Corpus-indexed anchor: Nature 2015 "Inequality and visibility of wealth in experimental social networks" (DOI:10.1038/nature15392, 376 cites, indexed).

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Status-signalling machinery is ancestrally calibrated; in an environment with mass-produced signals and global supply chains, the signal is still functional but the cost function has exploded.
- F2: ✓ Active purchase.
- F3: ✓ Individual: peer comparison locks in continued upgrading. Group: the bag's signalling value depends on other people's endorsement — a coordination equilibrium.
- F4: ✓ Net wealth effect is decades-deferred; the purchase dopamine is immediate.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** Universal. The Chinese case is distinctive only in speed of diffusion and per-capita intensity in specific cohorts.

---

## C6. 保健品 / 养生执念 — health-supplement and wellness over-consumption

**Phenomenology.** An elderly couple in a second-tier Chinese city attends a "free health lecture" at the community centre. The speaker demonstrates a ¥28,000 "quantum-water purifier". Over six months they buy three more devices, a vibrating foot-bath, and ¥50,000 of bee-pollen capsules. Their children discover only when the savings are depleted. Related Western phenomena include Goop, the supplement industry (Nature 2026 DOI:10.1038/d41586-026-00707-5 "What is the science behind 'science-backed' supplements?", indexed), and the wellness-retreat ecosystem.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Health-maintenance reward signal (a heuristic "I am taking care of myself") calibrated for real traditional-medicine signals; novel pseudo-scientific signals hijack it.
- F2: ✓ Active purchase, endorsed.
- F3: ✓ Individual: the "take care of yourself" identity locks in. Group: peer networks (elderly community groups, WeChat scam-targets) reinforce.
- F4: ✓ Real health outcomes lag by years; the "I did something for my health" reward is immediate.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** US supplement industry (~$50B annually); UK wellness industry; Japanese 健康食品; universal.

## C7. Multi-level marketing (MLM) / 传销

**Phenomenology.** A 31-year-old accepts a "business opportunity" from a friend: buy ¥10,000 of health products, recruit five more people, attend weekly motivational rallies. The upline tells her that in 18 months she will be financially free. She believes. She recruits her two cousins. At month 24, she has ¥80,000 of unsold inventory, her cousins blame her, and the promised "car" never arrives. The FTC and Chinese Ministry of Public Security have documented that >99% of MLM participants lose money, yet recruitment continues. Key mechanism: cognitive dissonance around the already-sunk investment combined with identity-investment in the "entrepreneur" frame.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Reward signal: group belonging + narrative of upward mobility. Ancestrally, "join the successful lineage" was a reasonable heuristic; in an MLM it is anti-calibrated.
- F2: ✓ Active endorsement — this is the signature feature. Participants defend the MLM to family.
- F3: ✓ Strong individual (sunk cost, identity) and group (rally culture, testimony structure) lock-in.
- F4: ✓ Financial loss appears only at exit; each day's rally delivers belonging.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** Amway (US origin, global); Herbalife; Chinese 传销 cases (and the state's repeated crackdowns); Japanese 連鎖販売取引. The Chinese "pure" (illegal) MLM is particularly notable because even after legal penalties the phenomenon persists — evidence of F4.

## C8. Investment FOMO — crypto, A-shares, 基金 fund mania

**Phenomenology.** During the 2021 Chinese mutual-fund boom ("明星基金经理" culture), millennial workers poured savings into celebrity fund manager products after 1-year returns of +80%. By 2023 most funds had given back the gains; the marginal investors entered at peak and lost 30–50%. Identical dynamic in 2017 and 2021 crypto bubbles. The corpus-indexed Nature 2023 editorial (DOI:10.1038/d41586-023-00140-y "Devise more controls to protect cryptocurrency investors") captures the regulatory framing.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Pattern-recognition reward (trend-following) calibrated for ancestral foraging (where recent success does predict near-future success); in financial markets it predicts the opposite (mean reversion).
- F2: ✓ Active investment.
- F3: ✓ Individual: averaging down, double-down escalation. Group: social-media trading communities create echo chambers.
- F4: ✓ Losses realised late; paper gains delivered continuously during the run-up.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** Universal. Charles Mackay's *Extraordinary Popular Delusions* (1841) catalogued tulip mania, the South Sea Bubble, and Mississippi Bubble with exactly this structure.

## C9. Knowledge-payment / 知识付费 FOMO

**Phenomenology.** A 28-year-old product manager pays ¥2,000/year for a "knowledge-planet" service, ¥500/month for a Himalaya audiobook subscription, and ¥8,000 for a three-month "career transformation" bootcamp. She listens at 2× speed on her commute, feels productive, and does not change her career. After three years she has spent ¥50,000, her career has not transformed, and she signs up for the next course. Chinese 知识付费 market was ≈¥700B in 2023.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Self-improvement signal (a heuristic "I am learning, I am progressing") that was calibrated for effortful mastery; hijacked by low-effort consumption of packaged content.
- F2: ✓ Active purchase.
- F3: ✓ Individual: identity lock-in ("I am a learner"). Group: peer-posting culture on social media.
- F4: ✓ Career outcome is decades-deferred; "I took a course" satisfaction is immediate.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** MasterClass (US), Udemy, Skillshare; universal. The Chinese case is distinctive in market size and in the degree to which the content is framed as elite knowledge.

## C10. 宗教/民间信仰过度奉献 — excessive religious/folk-belief donation

**Phenomenology.** An elderly woman in rural Fujian donates ¥100,000 of her savings to a local 大师 who promises to cure her grandson's cancer. The grandson dies. She interprets this as her donation being insufficient, and gives more. Analogous phenomena: televangelist donations in the US, prosperity-gospel adherents, cult-style commitments in Japan (Aum Shinrikyo) and Korea (Shincheonji). The Schlaepfer-Robertson "preference trap" framework applies directly.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Reciprocity and supernatural-patronage signals were calibrated in small-group religious contexts with accountable leaders; hijacked by scaled and unaccountable religious marketplaces.
- F2: ✓ Active endorsement.
- F3: ✓ Individual: religious identity lock-in, sunk-cost rationalisation of prior donations. Group: congregation social structure.
- F4: ✓ Outcome-feedback is systematically reinterpreted; the framework is unfalsifiable from the participant's perspective.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** Universal. The defining feature is the unfalsifiability of the reward-feedback structure, which is what makes F4 especially strong.

---

## C11. Sugar/fat/salt consumption — the Lieberman-paradigm human case

**Phenomenology.** A 35-year-old white-collar worker eats one bubble tea a day (~40g sugar), takeout for lunch (salt, saturated fat), and snacks in the evening. Per-sip satisfaction is real. By age 50 she has pre-diabetes, hypertension, and has been counselled three times by her doctor to reduce sugar. She reduces for two weeks and reverts. This is the canonical Lieberman evolutionary-mismatch case. Corpus-indexed anchors: Science 2016 "Exposing the exposures responsible for type 2 diabetes and obesity" (DOI:10.1126/science.aaf5094, 277 cites, indexed); Nature 2023 "Diabetes and obesity are rising globally" (DOI:10.1038/d41586-023-00676-z, indexed).

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Gustatory reward signal calibrated for ancestral calorie-scarcity. The Drosophila case (A5) is the cross-species parallel.
- F2: ✓ Active food choice.
- F3: ✓ Individual: dopaminergic habit formation and the Berridge "wanting vs liking" dissociation. Group: food-environment norms (office snack table, family meals).
- F4: ✓ Metabolic consequence 10–20 years deferred.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** Universal. The Chinese case is distinctive because it has unfolded in ~25 years (vs 50+ in the West) and is still in the early-stage uptake phase for some markets.

## C12. Short-video / mobile-game / gacha

**Phenomenology.** A 22-year-old spends 4.5 hours/day on Douyin and ¥3,000/month on a gacha mobile game, "pulling" for rare characters. The dopaminergic variable-ratio schedule is the purest laboratory of the Olds-Milner architecture outside a research setting. Corpus-indexed anchors: Nature 2024 "The great rewiring: is social media really behind an epidemic of teenage mental illness?" (DOI:10.1038/d41586-024-00902-2, 71 cites, indexed); the contested literature on teen mental health is itself evidence of the policy salience.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Variable-ratio reward signal (calibrated for ancestral foraging where variance in returns was real) hijacked by engineered schedules with zero real-world return.
- F2: ✓ Active engagement; users resist de-installation.
- F3: ✓ Individual: habit. Group: social-media norms (missed videos become social-exclusion).
- F4: ✓ Attention and sleep costs are delayed.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** Universal. The Chinese case is distinctive in short-video dominance (Douyin's format) and in gacha-mobile revenue concentration.

---

## C13. Conspicuous housing / automobile upgrading

**Phenomenology.** A young couple in Hangzhou buys a 120 m² apartment in a newly-developed school district, stretching the combined family down-payment ability (三代人的钱) and committing to a 30-year mortgage at 70% DTI. For two years after move-in they report elevated life satisfaction. In year 5 the school-district policy changes, the property value drops 20%, and the mortgage is underwater. They continue to make payments, their savings rate is near zero, they delay having a second child. The housing-status case is our strongest direct analogue of A4 jewel-beetle (supernormal signal trap) in the human domain.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Territorial and status-housing signals were ancestrally calibrated; urban real-estate markets provide supernormal versions.
- F2: ✓ Active purchase endorsement.
- F3: ✓ Individual: difficulty to downsize. Group: peer norms (the school-district floor).
- F4: ✓ Opportunity cost (forgone child, forgone career risk) is decades-deferred.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** Universal. The Chinese case is distinctive because housing is both a consumption and a status good and an investment — so all three primitives compound.

## C14. Relationship-consumption / 脱单焦虑 anxiety purchases

**Phenomenology.** A 29-year-old female accountant in Chengdu, pressured by her parents and peer group, joins three paid match-making services at ¥30,000–¥80,000 per package. Across 18 months she attends 40 first dates. The services use variable-ratio reinforcement (occasional compatible-seeming match) to maintain engagement. No stable relationship emerges. She continues because stopping is equated with "giving up".

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Mate-search reward signals hijacked by a novel market that produces supernormal signal density but low real match rate.
- F2: ✓ Active enrolment.
- F3: ✓ Individual: identity-investment in the search. Group: parental and peer pressure (the "left-over woman" 剩女 discourse).
- F4: ✓ Opportunity cost and financial loss lag.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** Chinese paid match-making is large-scale and quasi-industrial; Western dating-app industry (Tinder, Bumble, Hinge) uses the same variable-ratio architecture with subscription fees.

## C15. Fitness / body-transformation consumerism

**Phenomenology.** A 30-year-old male office worker joins a premium gym chain (¥8,000/year), supplements (¥500/month), a personal trainer (¥80,000/year), and an online coaching programme (¥3,000/month). His body composition improves marginally in year 1 and plateaus. At year 3 the cumulative spend is ¥300,000 and he has an injury that stops training. He renews his gym membership because stopping is identity-death.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Somatic investment signal calibrated for ancestral energy-balance; hijacked by marketised fitness industry.
- F2: ✓ Active subscription/purchase.
- F3: ✓ Identity + peer norm lock-in.
- F4: ✓ The feedback (absence of transformational results) is reinterpreted as "need to spend more".

**Cross-cultural dimension.** Universal; the US $100B+ fitness industry is the frontier case; China is catching up rapidly.

## C16. International-school / elite-education purchasing

**Phenomenology.** A Shanghai family enrols their child in an international school at ¥250,000/year with plans for overseas university at ¥500,000/year for four years. Total commitment: ~¥3–4M for one child's education. Parents report high satisfaction and social-network status. Many graduates eventually return to compete for the same domestic jobs as the non-international-school cohort. The marginal wage premium is often negative once the opportunity-cost of capital is counted.

**Feature check.**
- F1: ✓ Parental investment-in-offspring signal, calibrated for diminishing-returns environments, now applied to a zero-sum signalling environment.
- F2: ✓ Active enrolment.
- F3: ✓ Identity + peer lock-in.
- F4: ✓ Net returns are decades-deferred.

**Cross-cultural dimension.** US Ivy-feeder private-school market is the antecedent; Chinese international-school market has grown from near-zero to >$30B in 15 years.

*This phenomenon is a special case of C2 鸡娃 scaled up, but is listed separately because the cost structure is qualitatively different (overseas university) and the λ-externalisation channel (child's reverse-culture-shock, potential non-return migration trap) is distinct.*

# Section D. Explicitly excluded "false Sweet Traps"

Each entry: which feature fails, and why the exclusion sharpens the construct boundary.

## D1. 996 compulsory overwork

**Why excluded: F2 fails.** 996 is coerced exposure. Workers accept the hours because the alternative is termination; the formal offer is a non-negotiable contractual floor. At the *moment of choice* the worker does not actively believe that 72-hour weeks will produce a better life; they believe they will lose their job if they refuse. The aspirational-endorsement layer (which is 打鸡血 / 画饼, C1) may co-occur, but the 996 compulsion itself is coercion, not endorsement. Mis-categorising 996 as a Sweet Trap was the error Andy corrected on 2026-04-18.

**Construct-boundary insight.** F2 is a *strict* binary criterion; if a phenomenon is coerced at the decision moment, it is not a Sweet Trap even if it produces identical objective outcomes.

---

## D2. Cigarette / nicotine addiction

**Why excluded: F1 fails in a specific way.** The reward-fitness decoupling in addiction is not via ancestral-environment mismatch. Nicotine hijacks the reward system by direct pharmacological action on nAChR-mediated dopamine release; the decoupling is *biochemical* rather than *informational*. The Olds-Milner case (A6) is the boundary — we included it because it is the mechanistic archetype. But ordinary substance addictions in humans are better analysed with the Becker-Murphy 1988 "rational addiction" framework (see v1 construct document §2), in which the present-bias and tolerance structure do not require F1's mismatch pathway.

**Construct-boundary insight.** Sweet Trap lives in the *informational-decoupling* slice of the reward-miscalibration landscape; pharmacological addiction is an adjacent but distinct slice.

---

## D3. Gambling / thrill-seeking

**Why excluded: F3 is partial, and F1 is on ambiguous grounds.** Problem gambling is driven by the thrill of uncertainty, not by aspirational endorsement of a narrative. Participants do not typically defend gambling as "I believe this will make my life better in the long run"; they report being compelled, acknowledge it is irrational, and frequently self-identify as having a problem. The Gul–Pesendorfer 2001 "temptation and self-control" framework is the appropriate lens. Sweet Trap, by contrast, survives full disclosure — participants endorse the choice even when informed.

**Construct-boundary insight.** F2's "endorsement" is not merely the absence of coercion; it requires *subjective endorsement in the face of information*. If the agent says "yes I know it's bad, but I can't stop", that is temptation/compulsion, not Sweet Trap.

---

## D4. Pathological / compulsive shopping

**Why excluded: F1 fails.** Oniomania is a compulsion with an identified clinical profile (DSM-5 impulse-control category discussion). The reward-decoupling is neurochemical, not informational. Closely related to addiction.

**Construct-boundary insight.** The *rational-under-their-incentives* criterion excludes clinical compulsions.

---

## D5. Poverty trap

**Why excluded: F2 fails; the trap is structural, not reward-driven.** Classical poverty traps (Banerjee & Duflo; Dasgupta's *Economics of Biodiversity*) arise because fixed costs, credit constraints, and nutrition–productivity feedback loops produce multiple equilibria, the low one of which is welfare-reducing. The individual is not *endorsing* the low equilibrium; they are constrained into it. Sweet Trap requires active endorsement of the welfare-reducing option under (at least partial) choice.

**Construct-boundary insight.** Sweet Trap is a *behavioural-welfare-economics* construct at the individual level; structural poverty traps are a distinct mechanism operating at the economic-equilibrium level. A person can simultaneously be in a poverty trap and a Sweet Trap (e.g., a poor rural household in 彩礼-inflationary debt), but the two mechanisms are orthogonal.

---

## D6 (grey zone). Workaholism / intrinsic-reward overwork

**Boundary-case.** A surgeon, a researcher, an artist works 70 hours a week, loves the work, and is eventually separated from family/health. Is this a Sweet Trap?

- If the work is objectively meaningful (produces long-run welfare to self and others), F2 of the *objective welfare* test fails — it is not a trap.
- If the worker would, on full reflection, reallocate time, then it borders on Sweet Trap via F1 (aspirational-identity signal decoupled from holistic welfare) and F4 (health costs deferred).

**Verdict.** This is a genuine grey zone. We will *not* include it in the focal domains, and we will discuss it explicitly as a scope-limit case in the Stage 0β construct-boundary section.

---

## D7 (grey zone). Marriage / long-term relationship investment

**Boundary-case.** Relationship-maintenance investment is aspirationally endorsed, has long-run payoff uncertainty, and is socially normed. Why not Sweet Trap?

- F1 typically fails: the mate-search and pair-bonding reward signals are aligned (not decoupled) with long-run welfare for the majority of cases.
- F4 typically fails: feedback (relationship satisfaction) is continuous, not deferred.

**Verdict.** Sound marriages are not Sweet Traps. Specific *escalation* phenomena in the marriage market (bride-price C4, anxiety-consumption C14) are Sweet Traps.

**Pattern:**

# Section F. The three most persuasive cross-species parallel cases

These are the cases we should develop as the **narrative spine** of the Nature main / Science paper.

## F.1 Flight-to-flame + Fisherian runaway + 鸡娃 — the *same positive-feedback mechanism* at three scales

- **Moth**: individual-level sensory trap with fixed policy (no within-lifetime correction).
- **Peacock**: group-level coevolutionary runaway; individuals cannot escape because their mating depends on conformity to the inflated signal.
- **鸡娃**: group-level social-norm runaway; parents cannot escape because their child's marginal admission probability depends on conformity to the inflated investment floor.

All three have the structure: *reward rule + environmental/competitive novelty + positive-feedback amplification = equilibrium at which every individual is behaving rationally and the aggregate is destructive*. The mathematical kernel is identical (replicator dynamics on a coupled signal-preference system, or replicator dynamics on peer-comparison utility). This is the "one equation, three domains" narrative that buys a Nature main paper.

## F.2 Plastic ingestion + neonicotinoid-bees + sugar/fat/salt — the *signal-hijack* pathway

- **Plastic/turtle**: novel material mimics prey signature.
- **Neonicotinoid/bee**: novel molecule mimics nectar reward.
- **Sugar-fat-salt/human**: ancestral-appropriate gustatory signal, novel-environment super-abundance.

Three different species domains, one mechanism: a reward signal that evolved to track an adaptive correlate is coupled to a novel stimulus that decouples it. This is the Schlaepfer-Robertson "preference trap" generalised across biochemistry, sensory, and behavioural channels.

## F.3 Olds-Milner + livestream-tipping + MLM — *direct reward hijack without ancestral-environmental mimicry*

- **Olds-Milner**: electrode stimulates the reward circuit directly; no external signal at all.
- **Livestream tipping**: parasocial recognition signal has no ancestral analogue — it is pure novelty that plugs into the belonging-and-reciprocity circuitry.
- **MLM**: group-belonging and upward-mobility narratives are ancestrally-adaptive circuits; MLM content is a novel cultural signal with no adaptive correlate at all.

This cluster is critical because it shows Sweet Trap is *not* limited to the Lieberman "ancestral mismatch" framework. Some Sweet Traps operate via culturally novel signals that have no ancestral referent — they simply exploit general-purpose reward architecture. This is why the Stage 0β construct needs to broaden F1 beyond "ancestral mismatch" to "signal–fitness decoupling of any origin".

# Section G. Most distinctively Chinese phenomena with *Nature/Science*-level potential

The paper needs a cross-cultural claim, but it also needs a datable empirical anchor. The three most China-distinctive cases, with highest publication leverage, are:

## G.1 鸡娃 × 双减 policy (C2 × natural experiment)

- Natural experiment: July 2021 双减 policy abruptly outlawed for-profit tutoring for compulsory-education students.
- Identification: within-family DID comparing pre- and post-2021 spending and parental satisfaction.
- Novelty: we can show that a policy removed the equilibrium floor and generated a testable prediction (satisfaction up, child welfare up, parent savings up).
- This is publishable as a stand-alone at the level of QJE or AER, let alone as a case-study in a Sweet Trap synthesis.

## G.2 彩礼 × sex-ratio imbalance (C4)

- Bride-price inflation is the direct observable consequence.
- CFPS + CLDS longitudinal data allow within-family decomposition of the expenditure escalation.

## G.3 打鸡血/画饼 × tech-layoff cohort (C1)

- The 2022–2024 Chinese tech-layoff wave (Alibaba, Tencent, ByteDance) produced a natural experiment in forced disillusionment.
- Retrospective within-person analysis of life-satisfaction trajectories before, during, and after the end of the aspirational narrative.
- Identification: layoff timing is quasi-exogenous from the worker's perspective.
- This is exactly the "aspirational-endorsement versus coercion" test that distinguishes C1 from D1, and the timing provides causal leverage.

# Section H. Residual grey zones and unresolved questions for Stage 0β

1. **Does the construct require ancestral mismatch, or does cultural-novelty-only count?** Current decision: broaden F1. But this risks the construct becoming vacuous. Stage 0β must produce a rigorous formal criterion for "fitness-decoupled reward signal".
2. **The boundary with compulsion/addiction is fuzzy.** Olds-Milner is in. Cigarettes are out. What about sugar? Some sugar phenomena (A5 Drosophila, C11 ordinary diet) are clearly Sweet Traps; clinical binge-eating disorder is not. The operational test will need to key on subjective endorsement under full information.
3. **Group-level feature (F3) heterogeneity.** In some cases it is intra-generational peer-comparison (luxury, 鸡娃); in others trans-generational norm inheritance (彩礼); in others purely individual habit (Drosophila sugar); in others via mortality (moth to flame). A single model may not capture all sub-cases.
4. **Are we sure F2 endorsement is binary?** In 996 it clearly fails. In 打鸡血 it clearly holds. But many cases (C13 housing, C6 保健品) have mixed coercion/endorsement profiles. Stage 0β should consider F2 as a graded variable.

# Section I. Raw-material index for Stage 0β construct-building

What Stage 0β should abstract from this archive:

- **F1 (decoupling)** → formal model: define *fitness-weighted reward function* R_evo(s) and *agent's perceived reward function* R_agent(s); Sweet Trap requires *cor(R_evo, R_agent) ≤ 0* over the relevant signal distribution. This is the *informational* core and supersedes the "ancestral mismatch" narrower statement.
- **F2 (endorsement)** → behavioural axiom: agent's stated preference and revealed preference agree in favour of the sweet option at the moment of choice, under baseline information. Distinguishes from D1 (996) and D3 (gambling).
- **F3 (self-reinforcement)** → formal: *∂u_i/∂I_i > 0 conditional on E[I_{−i}]*, and *the equilibrium level of I* is strictly above the social optimum*. This is a standard coordination-failure structure.
- **F4 (absence of corrective feedback)** → dynamic: *the arrival time of punishment T_cost* is late relative to the decision horizon; and the information transmission mechanism between T_cost and T_decide is blocked (via externalisation, cohort separation, identity reinterpretation, or mortality).

**Recommended Stage 0β model architecture:** an **evolutionary game-theory core** (replicator dynamics on a population of agents with heterogeneous λ) coupled to a **behavioural-economics overlay** (agent utility includes {θ, β, ρ} as in v1 construct, but reinterpreted as the emergent parameters of the underlying dynamical system rather than as primitives). This gives us:

- Animal cases (A1–A11): pure evolutionary dynamics, λ = mortality/differential reproductive success, no behavioural overlay required.
- Human cases (C1–C16): evolutionary core + behavioural overlay; λ = inter-cohort cost transfer; β and ρ inherit from behavioural econ.

The unification of animal and human cases under a single mathematical framework — Lande-Kirkpatrick coevolutionary dynamics on the animal side, Bernheim-Taubinsky behavioural-public-economics on the human side, bridged by a shared "reward-fitness correlation" term — is the core theoretical contribution. This is what Nature main or Science wants: a framework that makes the same prediction for peacocks, moths, plastic-eating turtles, and parents buying piano lessons.
