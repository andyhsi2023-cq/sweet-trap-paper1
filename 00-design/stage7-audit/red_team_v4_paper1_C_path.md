## 0. Desk-Reject Probability at RSOS

**Overall desk-reject probability: 18–25%.**

RSOS's published criteria are *scientific validity + technical soundness + adequate reporting*; novelty/impact are explicitly NOT desk-screened. The C-path framing exploits this aperture well: a pre-registered universality test with three predictions failing is, by RSOS's own standards, a publishable falsification regardless of conclusion polarity. The risk is not "this isn't novel enough" (RSOS doesn't ask) but "the falsification is internally inconsistent" (RSOS does). Three live desk-reject vectors remain:

### Top-3 desk-reject candidates (ranked)

1. **Inter-rater reliability incomplete at submission (~10% probability of flagging)**. §3.1 and §4.6 admit blinded external three-coder recoding on the 30% subset is "not delivered in Paper 1." A pre-registered protocol committed Fleiss' $\kappa \ge 0.70$ as a coding gate, and the entire Layer-2 phylogenetic-signal claim rides on the case corpus being independently coded. RSOS editors *will* see this — the deviation is in the open. Whether they desk-reject or send to review depends on the handling editor's tolerance for "OSF addendum prior to peer review" as an adequate commitment. The risk is not zero.

2. **Layer-2 strong-falsification floor is essentially triggered, presented as not-triggered (~8% probability)**. §2.3 (line 94) sets the strong-falsification floor as "$K < 0.10$ AND $\lambda$ 95% CI includes 0." The observed $K = 0.117$ is 0.017 above the floor (within rounding); $\lambda$ CI = [0.0001, 0.413] *does* include zero. The manuscript at §3.2 acknowledges this ("the observation lies above the strong-falsification floor by 0.017") but reports the verdict as "REFUTED" rather than "STRONGLY REFUTED." A meticulous editor will ask whether the strong-falsification floor was meant as an *inclusive* or *exclusive* threshold; if inclusive, the verdict is more dramatic than the table conveys. This is unlikely to desk-reject by itself but compounds with #1.

3. **Pre-registration timing claim is internally inconsistent (~5% probability)**. §1 line 65: "we deposited a companion bioRxiv priority preprint (BIORXIV/2026/720498, 2026-04-24) and an OSF pre-registration (<https://osf.io/pv3ch>) concurrent with data extraction." But Deviation row #8 is dated 2026-04-25 and describes the *framing pivot* as occurring after the hostile audit. If pre-registration is "concurrent with data extraction" but the framing pivoted *after* the data was in hand, an editor reading carefully will ask: was the OSF pre-reg filed before the K=0.117 result was known? If the timestamp on osf.io/pv3ch is post-2026-04-24, the entire C-path "pre-registered falsification" framing is compromised. This is the single biggest narrative-integrity vulnerability and it is one OSF-page-screenshot away from being checkable by any reviewer.

## 1. Hostile Referee — First-Order Objections

### 1.1 The "lineage-specific origins" alternative is a post-hoc reframe with thinner clothes
**Passage**: §3.5 (line 227); §4.2 (lines 257–264); §4.6 (line 280, "Lineage-specific origins reading was not pre-registered as an alternative").
**Why it fails**: §4.6 admits this explicitly — the lineage-specific origins reading is "generated by the failure of the universal predictions" and "post-hoc and hypothesis-generating." Good. But §1 (line 55) states "The third treats it as repeatedly *convergent*, re-derived in lineages whose ecology decouples proximate signals from fitness, regardless of phylogeny." The §4.2 "lineage-specific origins" reading is **the same model** as the §1 third reading, only relabelled. So the manuscript pre-registers three universality readings, falsifies them, and then declares the survivor — which was sitting in §1 the whole time — as a discovered alternative. Stern 2013 and Losos 2011 (cited at §4.2 line 260) anticipate this reading; the manuscript does not pre-register a quantitative threshold under which the lineage-specific origins reading would itself fail. A *real* falsification framework would commit Paper 2 to a numeric prediction whose null could refute lineage-specific origins. The Limitations §4.6 commitment is a promissory note, not a pre-registration. **HARKing-after-null v2.0**: not as severe as the A-path version (the C-path correctly leads with refutation), but the post-hoc model is recoverable from the §1 trio without new theoretical work, which means the "test" was three vs one rather than three vs zero alternatives.

### 1.2 §3.6 "boundary case in *Homo sapiens*" demotes a SUPPORTED Layer-1 result by accident
**Passage**: §3.6 (line 229, title); Table 1 (line 234); Figure 1 caption (line 244).
**Why it fails**: Calling the human MR results a "boundary case" semantically downgrades what is empirically the strongest Layer-1 evidence in the paper (n=466 SNPs, OR 1.122, $p = 3.1 \times 10^{-31}$). "Boundary case" in evolutionary-biology usage means edge-of-clade or edge-of-effect — but here §3.6 uses it to mean "instantiation at one phylogenetic tip." This is a semantic confusion. Why is the result not in §3.1 with the rest of Layer-1? Putting it in a separately-numbered subsection at the end of Results, after the Verdict synthesis (§3.5), creates the visual impression that the human chains are a *side finding*, when in fact they are the most rigorous causal evidence in the paper. A reader who doesn't notice that §3.6 is human MR will think Layer 1 rests on the case-corpus PRISMA — which is the layer with two-coder IRR. The accidental effect is to weaken Layer 1.

### 1.3 The "boundary case" within Arthropoda ($K = 1.446$) cuts both ways
**Passage**: §3.2 (line 149); §4.2 line 262 ("the within-Arthropoda subset shows that the test does detect strong signal where ecological exposure clusters by clade").
**Why it fails**: The Arthropoda-only $K = 1.446$ is doing double duty. It defends against the Type II reading of the cross-phylum null (§4.2 line 262: "the test is not failing due to sample-size alone"). But within-Arthropoda $K > 1$ on $n = 13$ species is also exactly the pattern produced by **non-independence of ecological exposure** — Lepidoptera, Coleoptera, Hemiptera all share artificial-light-at-night vulnerability (§3.2 line 149 admits this). If the within-Arthropoda signal reflects shared ALAN exposure rather than shared phylogenetic descent of $\Delta_{ST}$, then it is not evidence that the test works on biological signal — it is evidence that the test detects ecological-niche clustering when present. PGLS on ALAN-exposure as a covariate (pre-registered §2.3 line 117 in earlier audit, here folded into §4.7) would distinguish these two interpretations and is not delivered. The defence against Type II is therefore weaker than §4.2 makes it sound.

### 1.4 The Pfam-Jaccard null is technically novel and undocumented enough to be re-checkable
**Passage**: §2.4 (line 101, "Sampling 10,000 random pairs of one-to-one orthologs of similar length and expression breadth from the same two reference genomes"); §3.3 (line 156, "$P(\text{Jaccard} = 0 \mid \text{null}) = 0.9998$").
**Why it fails**: $P = 0.9998$ is suspiciously precise and depends critically on (a) which Pfam version (A v37 vs B?), (b) the universe from which pairs are sampled (whole-genome ortholog set vs GPCR-only universe vs annotated-domain-bearing universe), (c) whether "one-to-one ortholog" filtering removes paralog pairs that would carry shared domain accessions. The supplementary methods don't appear to specify (a)–(c) at the level needed for a technical reviewer to re-derive 0.9998. A reviewer with a different defensible parameter set could easily get 0.95 instead of 0.9998 — and the substantive claim ("Jaccard = 0 is the modal outcome under the null") is **insensitive to the precise value** as long as it's > 0.5, but the *headline framing* in §3.3 ("exactly the modal expected outcome") and §4.4 ("exactly the modal expectation") leans on "modal" as a strong descriptor. If the reviewer's re-derivation gives 0.6 instead of 0.9998, "modal" is still technically correct but rhetorically weaker. A tighter universe (Class-A GPCRs only, n ~800) would likely give Jaccard > 0 in many random pairs and weaken the null further. The framing is robust to *its central claim* but vulnerable to *its presentation*.

### 1.5 5/6 LRT = 0.000 diagnostic is committed as a "Supplementary Figure S1 placeholder" but referenced as if delivered
**Passage**: §3.4 line 176 ("the randomised-starting-$\omega$ diagnostic listed as Supplementary Figure S1 placeholder"); §4.6 line 280 (same).
**Why it fails**: The diagnostic that distinguishes biological null from optimiser-boundary-convergence on 5 of 6 production clades is the difference between "5/6 clades give zero signal" and "5/6 clades' tests didn't fully run." The latter is a methodological invalidity for the 5/6 negative tests, not just a sensitivity check. RSOS's "technically sound" criterion arguably requires the diagnostic to be *run*, not committed. Submitting with the diagnostic as a "placeholder" is a fixable problem but it is a problem. Realistically: the diagnostic should be in the manuscript before submission, not as a supplementary placeholder. This is the single technical fix that would most strengthen the C-path.

### 1.6 *Apis* "tentatively supported" still does work the data may not warrant
**Passage**: §3.4 (line 176); §4.5 line 277; Verdict table line 225 ("**PARTIALLY REFUTED**" but classified as 1/6 tentative support).
**Why it fails**: The C-path framing improvement: "1/6 clades, lineage-specific exemplar" rather than "supported H6a." Honest. But §4.5 (line 273) still uses *Apis* as the *predicted signature* of the lineage-specific origins model — i.e., the post-hoc surviving theory makes a positive prediction (selection on Hymenopteran sweet receptors) that *Apis* fulfils. The structure is: (i) universal hypothesis fails on 5/6 clades; (ii) declare lineage-specific origins as the alternative; (iii) cite *Apis* as predicted by the alternative. This is structurally a Texas Sharpshooter — the bullseye is drawn around *Apis* after the fact. The alternative model would be just as predictive of *Drosophila Gr64* (sugar specialist) or *Aedes* (sugar-feeding mosquito) as it is of *Apis*. The model doesn't predict *Apis specifically*; it post-hoc accommodates *Apis* because that's the clade with signal. This concern is reduced but not eliminated by the §4.6 limitation that "lineage-specific origins reading was not pre-registered."

### 1.7 Title says "pre-registered cross-metazoan falsification" but only 3 of 4 layers were pre-registered as falsifiable
**Passage**: Title; Abstract line 40 ("We pre-registered three sharp predictions"); §1 line 63 ("We pre-register three falsifiable predictions"); §3.5 verdict table.
**Why it fails**: Layer 1 (existence) is not a pre-registered falsification — it's a measurement target ("$N \ge 50$ cases spanning $\ge 6$ phyla"). It can only be "supported" or "fail to be assembled," not falsified. Calling the paper a "pre-registered cross-metazoan falsification" is true for Layers 2–4 and not true for Layer 1. The title should arguably say "pre-registered universality falsification framework" or similar; "cross-metazoan falsification" implies that all four layers were under falsifiability-test, but Layer 1 was a corpus-construction target. Minor framing nitpick, but RSOS reviewers care about precision.

### 1.8 Layer-2 verdict ("REFUTED") under-states or over-states relative to the strong-floor criterion — pick one
**Passage**: §2.3 line 94 ("strong-falsification floor is $K < 0.10$ AND $\lambda$ 95% CI includes 0"); §3.2 line 145 ("$K = 0.117$ ... above the strong-falsification floor by 0.017"); Verdict table line 223 ("**REFUTED**").
**Why it fails**: The pre-registered scheme had two thresholds: ordinary falsification ($K \le 0.30$ OR $\lambda$ CI includes 0) and strong falsification ($K < 0.10$ AND $\lambda$ CI includes 0). Observed: $K = 0.117$, $\lambda$ CI includes 0. So $K \le 0.30$ is met (ordinary falsification triggered) and $\lambda$ CI inclusive of 0 is met. *Both* criteria for ordinary falsification are met; the strong floor is *missed by 0.017 on K* but met on $\lambda$. The verdict "REFUTED" is correct for the ordinary criterion. But §3.2 reports it without distinguishing ordinary-vs-strong — a reviewer who read §2.3 will want to see the distinction explicit, with a sentence like: "Ordinary falsification criterion (K ≤ 0.30 OR λ CI includes 0) triggered; strong-falsification floor (K < 0.10 AND λ CI includes 0) within rounding error on K alone." Without this explicit two-criterion accounting, the verdict label is doing argumentative work the data don't quite earn.

### Recommendation: **Major Revision**

### Summary for the Editor
The C-path pivot has substantively repaired the most damaging A-path flaws — the HARKing-after-null pattern is now openly disclosed; the molecular result is correctly framed as null-consistent rather than convergence-confirming; *Apis* is honestly downgraded to lineage-specific exemplar. The framework is internally more coherent than the A-path eLife submission. However, three issues remain: (i) the lineage-specific origins reading is post-hoc, salvaged from §1's third reading, and not yet bound to a falsifiable prediction; (ii) the IRR completion and 5/6 LRT = 0 randomised-$\omega$ diagnostic are committed as future work but should be delivered before submission; (iii) the OSF pre-registration timestamp must be verifiable as predating the data extraction, otherwise the entire C-path framing is compromised. These are remediable without re-execution.

## 3. Methodological Expert — Technical Audit on the 4 Pivot Questions

### Q1 — Did the pivot fix the A-path fatal flaws?

**A-flaw 1 (construct-molecular substrate mismatch)**: **Substantively fixed**. The C-path narrows H_universal_2 explicitly to TAS1R/Gr sweet-taste only (§2.4 line 96; §4.6 line 280) and defers photoreceptor / olfactory / opioid families to Paper 2 (Deviation row #7). The flagship cases of §1 (moths, turtles) are now framed as Layer-1 *existence* evidence (their $\Delta_{ST}$ contributes to the case corpus) rather than as Layer-3 *substrate* evidence (which is sweet-taste-specific). The bait-and-switch is closed. *Residual concern*: §1 line 53 still opens with moths-turtles-UPF; a reader skimming might still expect §3.3–§3.4 to test moth or turtle reward receptors. A clarifying sentence in §1 — "in this paper we test universality at the sweet-taste molecular substrate; other reward modalities are treated in Paper 2" — would seal the remaining gap. **Status: 85% resolved**.

**A-flaw 2 (HARKing-after-null on H3)**: **Mostly fixed; a softer post-hoc reframe persists**. The C-path explicitly leads with "$K = 0.117$ fails the pre-registered $K > 0.30$ prediction" (§3.2 line 145; §4.1 line 253; §4.2 line 258). The H3 null is reported as Layer-2 REFUTED, not as confirmation of convergence. This is a clean repair of the principal A-path flaw. **However**, §4.2 (line 260) and §4.5 (line 273) cite Stern 2013, Losos 2011, Speed & Arbuckle 2017 in support of the lineage-specific origins reading, which is the §1 third-reading recovered as the surviving alternative. The manuscript correctly classifies this as "post-hoc and hypothesis-generating" (§4.6 line 280), but a reviewer can argue: if the lineage-specific reading was already in §1 as one of three options, then the universality-null doesn't *generate* lineage-specific origins — it *eliminates two competitors*. So the §4.2 framing is closer to "by elimination" than "discovered alternative," and the manuscript could be more explicit about this. **Status: 75% resolved; HARKing-after-null v2.0 is a softer concern**.

**A-flaw 3 (Apis marginal signal as supported H6a)**: **Fixed**. §3.4 line 176 ("classify as **tentatively supported** rather than decisive"); §4.5 line 277 (caveats listed); Verdict table at §3.5 line 225 ("PARTIALLY REFUTED"); Deviation row #6 ("classified TENTATIVELY SUPPORTED rather than SUPPORTED"). The honest downgrade is consistent across abstract, results, discussion, and verdict. **Status: 95% resolved**.

### Q2 — Did the pivot introduce new flaws?

**Layer-4 verdict consistency**: The "PARTIALLY REFUTED" verdict (1/6 tentative; 5/6 LRT = 0) is **consistent with both the data and the pre-registered universality criterion** ("multiple production clades exceed Bonferroni"; §2.5 line 110). Pre-reg specifies 0/1-of-6 = falsification, single-clade signal = "lineage-specific, not universal." The verdict aligns. *No new flaw introduced* on Layer-4 framing.

**§3.5 lineage-specific origins as new post-hoc reframe**: As noted in Q1 above, the lineage-specific reading is sufficiently anticipated by §1 (third reading) that calling it "the most parsimonious surviving alternative" is defensible. §4.6 (line 280) discloses the post-hoc nature explicitly. **Disclosure is sufficient**; the *substantive* worry is the lack of a Paper-2 quantitative falsification criterion for lineage-specific origins itself. As written, lineage-specific origins is presented as harder-to-falsify than universality, which is a Lakatosian concern. *Mild new flaw, well-disclosed*.

**§3.6 demoting human MR**: Yes — by relegating the strongest causal evidence to a "Boundary case" subsection, §3.6 may **accidentally weaken the Layer-1 SUPPORTED verdict**. Recommend renaming §3.6 from "Boundary case: existence in *Homo sapiens*" to "Existence at the *Homo sapiens* tip (Layer-1 instantiation)" or similar — reframe from "boundary" to "instantiation." *Cosmetic but real new flaw*.

### Q4 — Methodological expert concerns specific to the questions

**Power on K at n = 56 and true $\lambda = 0.5$**: Münkemüller et al. 2012 (Fig 3 in *Methods Ecol Evol*) reports power ~40–60% for Blomberg's $K$ at n ≈ 50 and true $\lambda$ in (0.3, 0.7). The manuscript at §4.6 line 280 acknowledges this ("Blomberg's $K$ at $n \approx 56$ species has reduced power to detect $\lambda \in (0.3, 0.7)$"). §4.2 line 262 mitigates with internal evidence (within-Arthropoda K = 1.446 detection; replication across two subsets). **Adequacy**: the disclosure is in Limitations but not in Results. A reviewer may want a power-curve plot or formal posterior-predictive check on the $K$-power-vs-$\lambda$ surface. The manuscript could add a single sentence to §3.2 like "given Münkemüller-style power of ~50% at true $\lambda = 0.5$, the observed null is informative but not decisive in isolation; we triangulate against Layers 3–4 below." Currently this triangulation argument lives in §4.2; moving a sentence forward would make the Type-II disclosure harder to miss. **Status: disclosed but back-loaded**.

**Pfam-null parameter defensibility**: $P(\text{Jaccard} = 0 \mid \text{null}) = 0.9998$. The defensibility depends on (a) Pfam version (current: A v37, likely; not stated in §2.4 or §3.3); (b) sampling universe — *one-to-one orthologs of similar length and expression breadth* (§2.4 line 101) — but "similar" is unbounded; (c) k1, k2 = number of vertebrate / insect proteins per pair — a reviewer would want to see this parameterised. A tighter universe (Class-A GPCR-only, n ~ 800) would likely yield Jaccard > 0 in many random pairs and weaken P(=0|null) below 0.99 toward 0.6–0.8. **The substantive claim ("non-shared, not shared, molecular substrate") survives any defensible parameter choice** because Jaccard = 0 between TAS1R and Gr is a hard zero, not a near-zero. But the *headline number 0.9998* is sensitive to assumptions. Recommend Supplementary Methods specify Pfam-A v37 (or whichever), state the explicit ortholog-pool definition, and report sensitivity to (a)/(b)/(c). **The C-path is robust on substance, vulnerable on the precise rhetorical figure**.

**Apis n=4 + ω=36.2 boundary risk**: The framing "tentatively supported / boundary risk" (§3.4 line 176) is **strong enough**. §4.5 line 277 adds the BUSTED/aBSREL/RELAX commitment for Paper 2. *However*, BUSTED on an n=4 alignment will itself struggle (BUSTED is robust on n ≥ 6, ideally n ≥ 10); deferring triangulation to Paper 2 with expanded *Apis*–*Bombus*–*Vespa* n ≥ 10 is the right move. The *Apis* signal will either replicate or vanish on the larger taxon set; either outcome is informative. **Status: framing adequate**.

**5/6 LRT = 0 randomised-$\omega$ diagnostic — supplementary placeholder**: This is the **single technical-soundness concern that should be addressed before submission, not as a placeholder**. The diagnostic distinguishes "biological null" from "optimisation failure"; in the latter case, 5/6 of the production tests are *invalid*, not negative. Submitting with this committed-but-undelivered will draw a focused reviewer comment, and the editor may pause the review pending its delivery. Run time: ~4–8 CPU-hours given existing PAML setups (one re-run per clade × 5 starting-$\omega$ values × 5 clades = 25 runs). **Recommendation: deliver before submission, not after**.

### Verdict
**Needs patching** (not re-execution). Six items: (1) deliver Fleiss' $\kappa$ on 30%-subset; (2) run randomised-starting-$\omega$ diagnostic on 5 LRT = 0 clades; (3) specify Pfam-null parameter universe in Supplementary Methods; (4) move Type-II power disclosure forward into §3.2; (5) rename §3.6 from "Boundary case" to "Existence at *Homo sapiens* tip"; (6) add explicit sentence in §1 narrowing molecular evidence to sweet-taste only, with other modalities deferred to Paper 2. None require re-execution; all are revision-class.
