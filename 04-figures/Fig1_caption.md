# Figure 1 — Sweet Trap Conceptual Framework

**Figure 1. Sweet Trap: a universal reward–fitness decoupling construct, its axiomatic
foundations, and the four-part evidence programme testing its evolutionary origins.**

**(a) Cross-phyla instances of Sweet Trap.** Six representatives spanning six phyla
demonstrate that reward–fitness decoupling under voluntary endorsement is not
taxonomically restricted. From top-left: *Lepidoptera* (Arthropoda) — phototactic
approach to artificial light culminating in lethal collision; *Passeriformes* (Chordata)
— nest construction with anthropogenic plastic material despite reduced hatching
success; *Homo sapiens* (Chordata) — ultra-processed food consumption endorsed
despite measurable metabolic consequence; *Aplysia* spp. (Mollusca) — preferential
approach to hyper-caloric food stimuli beyond energetic requirement; *Caenorhabditis
elegans* (Nematoda) — preference for favoured food cues in contexts that reduce
reproductive fitness; *Nematostella vectensis* (Cnidaria) — reward-cue approach
behaviour producing maladaptive response. In every case, the signed scalar
Δ_ST = *U*_perc − E[*U*_fit | *B*] > 0: the proximate reward signal (perceived utility)
exceeds the fitness-conditional expectation. The red separator bar at the bottom of
the panel emphasises that this wedge is the shared feature across all six phyla.

**(b) Axiomatic framework and the Δ_ST scalar.** Two overlapping probability
distributions illustrate the environmental-decoupling mechanism (Axiom A2). The grey
dashed curve (*S*_anc) represents the ancestral signal environment in which reward
perception was calibrated by natural selection to track fitness (Axiom A1: *U*_perc
= φ(*s*, **g**) monotonically encodes *U*_fit). The blue solid curve (*S*_mod) represents
the modern or engineered signal environment after separation time *t*_sep. The red wedge
between distribution peaks is Δ_ST = *U*_perc − E[*U*_fit | *B*]: the organism perceives
maximal reward at the *S*_mod peak, while fitness expectation remains anchored near the
*S*_anc peak. Axiom A3 (endorsement inertia: behaviour maximises (1 − *w*)*U*_perc +
*w*E[*U*_fit | *B*] with *w* < 1/2, approaching 0 for non-human animals) and Axiom A4
(partial cost visibility: *c*_eff(τ) = δ(τ) · *c*, δ′ < 0) are annotated on the right.
All values shown are symbolic and illustrative; no empirical data are represented in
this panel.

**(c) Four-part evidence architecture.** Horizontal banner summarising the four empirical
parts of the research programme, each testing one biological question. **Part 1**
(blue) asks whether Sweet Trap exists in humans with measurable behavioural,
physiological, and genetic signatures, using trans-ancestry Mendelian randomisation
across five ancestries and six cohorts (key pre-registered statistic: pooled β ≥ 0.10,
H1). **Part 2** (green) asks how broadly Sweet Trap is distributed across Animalia,
using PRISMA meta-analysis of ≥ 50 animal cases across 6 phyla with phylogenetic-signal
tests (key statistic: Blomberg's *K* ≥ 0.30, H2 + H3 + H5). **Part 3** (pink) tests
whether reward signalling is produced by convergent functional architecture across
phyla — non-orthologous but domain-equivalent receptor families — with within-phylum
purifying selection as positive control (key test: cross-phylum InterPro Jaccard
≥ 0.70, H4a + H4b). **Part 4** (red) tests for lineage-specific positive selection
on reward-receptor ligand-binding domains in ecological-shift lineages and synthesises
the cross-species genetic-manipulation literature (key statistic: branch-site LRT
significant on ≥ 3/15 genes, H6a + H6f). Coloured arrows between panels indicate
the direction of the argument from construct (Panel a) through operational scalar
(Panel b) to empirical tests (Panel c). All predicted effect sizes and falsification
criteria are pre-registered on OSF prior to data extraction.
