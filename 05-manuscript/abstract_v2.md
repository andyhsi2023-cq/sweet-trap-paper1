# Abstract (v2) — Nature Human Behaviour

**Word count:** 298 / 300 ceiling (NHB structured 200–300 word).

---

**Background.** When a reward signal that once tracked survival and reproduction instead tracks a modern cue — sugar, ornament, algorithmic feed, leverage — continued endorsement of that signal can harm the chooser. We test whether this "Sweet Trap" pattern is a single, phylogenetically universal equilibrium rather than a collection of domain-specific phenomena.

**Methods.** We integrate four evidence layers: (A) a pre-registered random-effects meta-analysis of 20 non-human animal cases spanning seven taxonomic classes and four mechanism categories; (B) specification-curve analyses across five human domains — investment, diet, short-video use, housing leverage, alcohol — covering 3,000 alternative model specifications; (C) 17-wave ISSP aspirational-attitude trajectories across 25 countries (n = 2.9 M individual records); and (D) 19 Mendelian-randomisation chains in UK Biobank-instrumented exposures (n = 258,000–1,331,000) against FinnGen R12 outcomes (n ≈ 413,000). We anchor these layers to global health burden via GBD 2021.

**Findings.** The reward-fitness decoupling gradient is positive and convergent across all 20 animal cases (pooled Δ_ST = +0.645 [+0.557, +0.733], I² = 85%) and shows no vertebrate–invertebrate difference (p = 0.70). Mechanism ranks — reward hijack > sensory exploitation > Fisher runaway — are identically recovered in animals and in human genetic-causal data (Spearman ρ(A, D) = +1.00; A+D-only gradient β = +1.58, p = 0.019). In humans, spec-curve medians confirm Sweet-Trap signatures for housing leverage (β = +0.243), alcohol (β = +0.134), and investment FOMO (β = −0.077), with diet and short-video exposure directionally consistent but power-limited. Mendelian-randomisation establishes causal welfare-reducing chains at scale: BMI → type-2 diabetes (OR = 2.06); alcohol → alcoholic liver cirrhosis (OR = 5.41); risk tolerance → anxiety (OR = 1.63). Integrated across chains, Sweet Trap exposures account for ~34.6 million disability-adjusted life years per year globally [16.2 – 64.1 M] — approximately ten times the annual burden of Parkinson's disease.

**Interpretation.** Reward-fitness decoupling behaves as a single cross-species equilibrium whose animal-observed mechanism gradient predicts human genetic causation. The construct is classified by two necessary conditions (F1 decoupling, F2 endorsement without coercion) achieving 100% accuracy on ten adversarial boundary cases. Interventions that reshape the signal distribution — not information campaigns alone — are implied to be the dominant policy lever.
