Moths pursue artificial lights; sea turtles eat plastic; humans aspirationally over-consume investment leverage, short-video, mortgages, alcohol. We introduce **Sweet Trap**: reward–fitness decoupling under aspirational endorsement. Four axioms define a signed wedge Δ_ST between perceived and fitness utilities; a central theorem derives the bound |Δb_signal|/|Δb_info| ≥ 1.5 on intervention asymmetry under dose-matched comparison. A four-layer test combines a 20-case animal meta-analysis (pooled Δ_ST = +0.645), 19 Mendelian-randomisation chains (BMI→T2D OR = 2.06), ISSP trajectories across 25 countries, and 3,000 pre-registered specifications. A pre-registered cross-level joint test gives β = +1.58, p = 0.019; median intervention-asymmetry ratio across six domains = 11×. An F1+F2 binary classifier validates at Cohen's κ = 1.00 on 18 blind-coded cases. Signal-redesign interventions structurally outperform information within Sweet Trap domains; not elsewhere.
