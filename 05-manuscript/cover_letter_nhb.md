**To the Editor**
*Nature Human Behaviour*

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, **"Sweet Trap: a cross-species reward–fitness decoupling equilibrium with a Steiger-correct welfare anchor of 4.1–34.6 million DALYs per year globally"**, for consideration as an Article in *Nature Human Behaviour*.

**Why now.** Three policy streams converge on mechanisms our manuscript formalises. The WHO ultra-processed food review (2024) and the UK sugar-tax decadal evaluation (2024–2025) are the first population-scale tests of signal-distribution rather than information-based health policy. The EU Digital Services Act (2024–) mandates algorithmic-recommender transparency without a mechanism-level framework for why variable-ratio feeds produce the welfare losses the Act seeks to contain. In parallel, China's 2024 telecom-fraud crackdown (437,000 cases in 2023) and the FBI IC3's $4.57 billion confidence-fraud statistics describe a rapidly-expanding engineered-deception category that classical behavioural frameworks do not cleanly classify. This manuscript supplies the architectural account that unifies these policy objects under one pre-registered cross-species equilibrium.

**Three top-level claims are tested empirically.** First, we validate an **F1 + F2 necessary-sufficient classifier** of Sweet Traps on 18 cases (10 development + 3 held-out Positives + 2 Engineered-Deception extensions + 3 surface-similar Negatives). Binary Cohen's κ = 1.00 [95% CI 0.65, 1.00] across two independent blind coders, with quadratic-weighted κ ≈ 0.86 on 48 ordinal cells — the first empirically-validated falsification-ready construct of reward-fitness decoupling. Second, a **pre-registered cross-level mechanism-rank test** (A+D subset frozen 2026-04-17 before analysis) shows the animal-mechanism gradient predicts the human genetic-causal gradient at β = +1.58, p = 0.019 on the z-scored effect scale. The full three-layer model returns p = 0.47; we report both, with the pre-registered secondary as headline inferential evidence. Third, linkage through 19 Mendelian-randomisation chains to GBD 2021 population-attributable fractions places the **welfare anchor at 4.1–34.6 million DALYs per year globally** — the Steiger-correct floor equivalent to Parkinson's disease, the extended envelope approximately 10×. Both are reported side-by-side with full methodological rationale.

**Why NHB.** The manuscript is structurally cross-species (20 animal cases across 7 taxonomic classes + 5 human focal domains + 54 countries + 19 MR chains), construct-building (F1+F2 classifier with pre-registered Engineered Algorithmic vs Engineered Deception sub-classes), and welfare-anchored (GBD-linked DALY estimate). This combination of phylogenetic breadth, construct-validation rigour, and population-scale welfare quantification fits *Nature Human Behaviour*'s behavioural-science scope most naturally. We do not propose a handling editor at this stage and welcome editorial guidance.
