**To the Editor**
*Nature Human Behaviour*

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, **"Sweet Trap: a theory of reward–fitness decoupling with cross-species empirical validation"**, for consideration as an Article in *Nature Human Behaviour*.

**Contribution.** We formalise an axiomatic theory of reward–fitness decoupling (the **Sweet Trap**: four axioms, four theorems, one keystone scalar Δ_ST = *U*_perc − E[*U*_fit ∣ *B*]) and validate it across a four-layer empirical programme: 20 non-human animal cases (seven taxonomic classes), five human focal domains with 3,000 pre-registered specifications, 25 ISSP countries (*n* = 2.9 M), and 19 Mendelian-randomisation chains against FinnGen R12. The central new result — **Theorem T2 (Intervention Asymmetry)** — proves that signal-redesign interventions structurally dominate information interventions by at least (1 − *w*_max)/*w*_max ≥ 1.5 under dose-matched comparison. This is a theorem, not an empirical generalisation. We test its prediction P1 across six focal domains: unit-matched ratios (C8: 74×; C12: 7×) clear the 1.5 floor, with signal-redesign dominating information in 6/6 domains. An F1+F2 binary classifier validates at κ = 1.00 on 18 blind-coded cases.

**Why now.** Three concurrent policy streams converge on mechanisms this paper formalises. The WHO ultra-processed-food review (2024) and UK sugar-tax decadal evaluation (2024–2025) are the first population-scale tests of signal-distribution rather than information-based health policy. The EU Digital Services Act (2024–) mandates algorithmic-recommender transparency without a mechanism-level framework for variable-ratio-driven welfare losses. China's 2024 telecom-fraud crackdown (437,000 cases in 2023) and the FBI IC3's USD 4.57 billion confidence-fraud statistics describe an engineered-deception category classical behavioural frameworks do not cleanly classify. Our axiomatic construct supplies the unifying architectural account with an empirically testable intervention-asymmetry theorem.

**Honest limitations.** Of five theorem-derived predictions: **P1** strongly supported; **P3** partially supported (ISSP R² = 0.255, above the 0.15 falsification floor, below the 0.40 target); **P5** reported as empirical regularity consistent with T3 (ψ-commensurability premise not formally established); **P4** qualitatively consistent; **P2** awaits dedicated empirical test. The three-layer A+B+D meta-regression returns *p* = 0.47; the pre-registered A+D subset (β = +1.58, *p* = 0.019) is the headline inferential evidence. The C13 housing anomaly is reported transparently without mechanism-reclassification.

**Fit.** The manuscript's combination of phylogenetic breadth (seven taxonomic classes + 25 countries + 19 MR chains), formal axiomatic theory, pre-registered empirical validation, and a construct-derived policy-predictive theorem fits *Nature Human Behaviour*'s scope most naturally. We do not propose a handling editor and welcome editorial guidance.
