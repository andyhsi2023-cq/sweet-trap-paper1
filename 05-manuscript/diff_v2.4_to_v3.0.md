## Top-level structural changes

1. **Title reframed.** v2.4: "Sweet Trap: a cross-species reward–fitness decoupling equilibrium **and a derived law of intervention effectiveness**". v3.0: "Sweet Trap: **a theory of reward–fitness decoupling with cross-species empirical validation**". Rationale: "law" overclaim flagged in Paper 1 Phase D audit; v3.0 title advertises theory + empirics as co-equal contributions without "law" language.

2. **New §2 Theory section inserted (~1,200 words).** v2.4 had no axiomatic theory; §1 "Sweet Trap framework" was a 100-word F1–F4 description. v3.0 §2 contains: §2.1 primitives (choice space, dual utilities, Δ_ST keystone); §2.2 four axioms A1–A4 with A3.0 scope criterion and A4-P1 parameterization explicitly marked (Stage 1-B revisions); §2.3 four theorems T1–T4 with T4 basin-radius demoted to Observation 4.1 (Stage 1-B M5); §2.4 three sub-classes (MST/RST/EST); §2.5 positioning against seven adjacent theories (PT, RA, Mismatch, Eco Trap, Fisher, Nudge, Dual System).

3. **Results section reframed as theory-test.** Every Layer (A, B, C, D, cross-level, P1) now explicitly indicates which axiom/theorem/prediction it validates. Layer A validates T3 universality + A2 decoupling; Layer B validates A2 + A3.0 in 5 human domains; Layer C validates P3 (RST cultural amplification); Layer D validates causal architecture of A2 + sub-class specificity; cross-level A+D validates P5 (as empirical regularity consistent with T3, not theorem-derived — Stage 1-B M4); Fig. 8 intervention-asymmetry compilation validates P1 (theorem-derived from T2).

4. **§8 "Policy predictability law" → §3.6 "P1 intervention-asymmetry test".** v2.4 §8 presented intervention asymmetry as a construct-derivative empirical pattern. v3.0 §3.6 presents it as the theorem-derived prediction P1 against T2. The Table 1 × 6-domain content is preserved verbatim.

5. **Abstract rewritten.** v2.4 was empirical-only (Background / Methods / Findings / Interpretation). v3.0 is structured as Background → Theory → Empirics → Prediction status → Implications, with honest P1/P2/P3/P4/P5 status reporting.

6. **Methods reorganised.** v2.4 M1–M13 focused on empirics. v3.0 Methods: §M1 Theory (full axioms, theorems, lemmas — new, ~1,200 words); §M2–M5 empirical (carried from v2.4 with renumbering); §M6 intervention-asymmetry (= v2.4 M10); §M7 discriminant-validity (= v2.4 M8); §M8 pre-registration and transparency (consolidates v2.4 M12 + M13).

7. **Cover letter rewritten (cover_letter_nhb_v3.md).** Core claims: (1) Theory: axiomatic Sweet Trap (four axioms, four theorems, keystone Δ_ST). (2) Empirical: four-layer validation + pre-registered A+D β = +1.58 p = 0.019 + P1 intervention-asymmetry confirmed against T2. (3) Policy: T2 reframes why information campaigns underperform. Avoids "first empirically-validated" and "law of X" language.

8. **SI restructured.** v2.4 SI outline was 9 appendices (A–H + §11.7b PUA). v3.0 SI has two blocks: **SI-Math** (9 sections of theoretical supplement, from Paper 1 axioms_and_formalism.md / theorems.md / lemmas.md / positioning.md; ~3,500 words) and **SI-Empirical** (A–I, carried from v2.4; Appendix F intervention-asymmetry sources retained; Appendix H DALY accounting retained as orthogonal descriptive statistic, not primary claim; Appendix I PUA boundary case unchanged).
