# Highlights

- **Layer 1 (existence) — SUPPORTED.** A PRISMA-ScR-screened corpus of **114 cases / 7 phyla / 56 species** satisfies the F1–F4 operational criteria for reward-fitness decoupling, doubling the pre-registered ≥50-case target.
- **Layer 2 (phylogenetic signal) — REFUTED.** Cross-phylum Blomberg's $K = 0.117$ ($p = 0.251$, $\lambda \approx 0$) does not meet the pre-registered $K > 0.30$ threshold; the universality-as-inheritance reading of Sweet Trap is rejected.
- **Layer 3 (shared molecular substrate) — NOT SUPPORTED.** Vertebrate TAS1R and insect Gr sweet-receptor families share zero Pfam domains (Jaccard = 0); against a matched-random Pfam null baseline, $P(\text{Jaccard} = 0\,|\,\text{null}) = 0.9998$ — exactly the modal null outcome and not statistical convergence evidence.
- **Layer 4 (universal positive selection) — INCONCLUSIVE — optimiser-sensitive.** After randomised-starting-$\omega$ diagnostic [@anisimova2007branchsite] on all 6 production clades: 3/6 robustly null, **3/6 optimiser-sensitive** (*Apis* Gr_sweet, *Drosophila* Gr64 cluster, *Drosophila* all-Grs — all show boundary-escape behaviour at one or more $\omega_0$ starts), **0/6 robust positives**; all three non-zero signals lie in the small-clade boundary-artefact range ($\omega > 30$ on $n \le 7$ taxa). The @baldwin2014hummingbird hummingbird TAS1R1 control reproduces at LRT = 55.9 ($p < 4 \times 10^{-14}$); the failure mode is insufficient method power on this alignment, not confirmed absence of selection.
- **Synthesis.** Sweet Trap is a **widespread but not universal** phenomenon — supporting a *lineage-specific origins* model in which reward-fitness decoupling re-emerges wherever local ecological conditions decouple proximate signals from realized fitness, on whatever reward-receptor machinery the lineage has available. Paper 2 will identify the triggering ecological conditions on an independent corpus.
